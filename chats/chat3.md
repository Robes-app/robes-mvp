Prompt — what's the occasion, where are you going, what's the weather, what time of day. Everything downstream responds to this. This is the brief. It's also exactly what Robes takes as the prompt input.
2. Dress code. Formality tier. Not just black tie vs casual — the subtler spectrum matters more. Smart casual at a rooftop vs a garden party vs a work dinner are three different languages even if none are formal.
3. The base. Well-fitting pieces in good fabric. These are the edit, not the statement. Most outfits fail here — a great piece in a bad fit, or the right silhouette in a fabric that doesn't hold its shape. This is the unglamorous work that makes everything else possible.
4. Colour. One dominant, one supporting, one accent maximum. The mistake is trying to do too much. A strong neutral base plus one deliberate colour moment beats five competing ones every time. Skin tone matters here too — Robes captures this in onboarding for a reason.
5. Proportion. Volume and silhouette balance. Relaxed top, fitted bottom. Or structured blazer, fluid trouser. The eye needs somewhere to land and somewhere to rest. Hem length vs shoe height, where the waist sits — these decisions are what separate a considered outfit from just wearing clothes.
6. The editorial layer. The something nice. One piece that elevates the whole thing — a specific bag, a shoe with intention, the right jewellery, an unexpected texture. This is the layer people remember. It's also the layer most women either skip entirely (no time) or over-do (two competing things). One. That's it.
